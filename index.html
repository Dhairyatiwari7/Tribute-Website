A.P.J. Abdul kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India.

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, India, was a distinguished scientist known as the "Missile Man of India" for his pivotal role in India's missile and space programs. As the 11th President of India (2002-2007), he emphasized education, , and youth empowerment. Kalam's vision for India, outlined in his book "India 2020: A Vision for the New Millennium," aimed at transforming the country into a developed nation through innovation and human capital. His leadership and humility earned him the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, leaving a lasting legacy as a role model and inspiration for generations, particularly in the fields of science, education, and national development.
